# Disclaimer and corrections policy

Last updated: 27 June 2026

Please read this before relying on anything published by Tender Watch. By using this
tool or its data you accept the terms below.

## What Tender Watch is

Tender Watch is an independent, non commercial, public interest project. It cleans
already public data from India's Central Public Procurement Portal (CPPP) and presents
statistics and indicators about public procurement, so that the information is easier
to examine.

## What the flags are, and what they are not

The flags, scores, and indicators on this site are **statistical signals, not findings
of fact and not accusations of wrongdoing**. A flag means only that a record has a
characteristic worth a closer human look, for example a single bidder, a short bidding
window, or a missing value. There are routine, lawful, and innocent explanations for
almost every flag.

Nothing here should be read as a statement that any organisation, company, official, or
individual has acted improperly, illegally, or dishonestly. No such claim is made or
intended.

## Not professional advice

The content is provided for general information only. It is not legal, financial,

## Accuracy and limitations

The underlying data is scraped from a public source and is imperfect. In particular:

- Coverage of tender notices is partial, so several flags apply only to a subset of
  awards and understate reality.
- Contract values are missing or unreliable for many public sector bodies.
- Company name matches against the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) register are by
  name only and probabilistic. They are shown only for unambiguous matches and are
  labelled as likely matches to verify, never as confirmed links.
- Company status from MCA is a present day snapshot with no historical date, so a
  current status does not describe the position at the time of any award.
- The source contained duplicate records (the same award scraped more than once). These
  are de-duplicated, but a small residue of near-duplicates with missing identifiers is
  kept on purpose, so counts may be very slightly overstated.
- Some records contain scraping or formatting errors.

The full set of limitations is described on the Methodology page and in the project
documentation. Always verify a record against the original before acting on it or
reporting it. Note that records identified by a standard CPPP Tender ID can be searched
on the official portal, but many public sector awards (for example NTPC) carry only an
internal reference number, which the central portal does not let you search. Those are
best verified on the data source mirror or by searching the title and organisation.

## Identification of named parties

Where a company or party is named, that name is reproduced from public records. The
same or a similar name may refer to more than one entity, and a name in the procurement
data may not be the same legal person as a similarly named company in the MCA register.
Treat every identity as requiring independent confirmation.

## Corrections, complaints, and removal requests

We want the data to be accurate and fair. If you are an affected party, or you spot an
error, you can ask for a correction or removal.

- How to raise one: open an issue on the project's GitHub repository at
  **https://github.com/abcde-stack/tender-watch/issues**. Creating a GitHub account is
  free.
- Please include the specific record (the Tender ID or Reference Number and the page),
  what you believe is wrong, and any supporting information.
- Note that GitHub issues are public. Do not post sensitive personal information in an
  issue; describe the problem and the record, and that is enough for us to act.
- We aim to acknowledge requests within a reasonable time and to correct or remove
  genuine errors promptly. Where appropriate we will annotate a record rather than
  silently change it, so the correction is visible.

Raising a correction request does not by itself mean the underlying public record will
change, because the source data is published by third parties and by government.

## Personal data

This project avoids publishing personal data wherever possible. For example, the email
address field present in the MCA dataset is never displayed or republished. If you
believe personal data has been published in a way that affects you, use the contact
above and we will address it.
